02 — Features

Three modules.
One seamless experience.

📍 Module 1
01

Find — Hub Locator

Live map of every 3MTT hub, cybercafé, WiFi zone, and public library across all 774 LGAs. Filter by Free WiFi, Printing, Government services, or 🤟 Deaf-Friendly badge. Offline-first, cached locally. Available in Pidgin, Yoruba, Hausa, and Igbo.

🤟 Module 2
02

Learn — NSL Lessons

Video-based Nigerian Sign Language lessons for deaf fellows, hub staff, and hearing users. Gamified with XP, streaks, and verifiable certificates. Offline-first. Separate tracks for Student, Staff, and Family modes.

🌉 Module 3
03

Bridge — Sign-to-Text Communication

Real-time sign language recognition using the phone camera + TensorFlow Lite AI. Deaf users sign → BridgePoint translates to text for staff. Staff type → BridgePoint displays sign guidance back. No interpreter. No internet required.

🔕 Offline-First
04

Zero Data. Full Features.

The hub map, all NSL videos, the Bridge sign library, and the AI recognition model all cache locally after first download. A deaf 3MTT fellow in a rural LGA can use every feature of BridgePoint with zero mobile data.

🏅 Deaf-Friendly Badge
05

Hub Accessibility Ratings

Hubs whose staff complete the NSL Staff Track earn a verified Deaf-Friendly badge on the map — creating an incentive loop. Deaf fellows choose badged hubs. Hubs train staff to get badged. Inclusion becomes self-reinforcing.

🌍 5 Languages
06

Built for Every Nigerian

Full UI in English, Pidgin, Yoruba, Hausa, and Igbo. NSL video lessons. Voice output for low-literacy users. High contrast mode and haptic feedback for users with additional visual impairments. Inclusion is the foundation — not a feature.

The full BridgePoint journey

1

Fatima finds her nearest hub offline

She opens the Find tab with zero data. The cached map loads instantly. She filters by 🤟 NSL to find a Deaf-Friendly hub and gets walking directions — no internet needed at any point.

2

Emeka opens Bridge Mode at the hub entrance

He taps the 🌉 Bridge tab and selects Sign → Text. The camera activates. The on-device NSL-TFLite AI model begins detecting his hand shape in real time — no data, no server call.

3

He signs his message — word by word

Each sign is detected with a confidence score. He taps "Add to sentence" to build his full message in the Sentence Builder. When complete, he taps "Send to Staff" — the full translated sentence appears on the hub coordinator's screen.

4

Staff replies in Text → Sign mode

The coordinator switches to Text → Sign, types "Welcome! Please take a seat." BridgePoint instantly displays the corresponding NSL sign guide — each word shown as an animated emoji-based hand shape for Emeka to read.

5

Staff learns NSL — hub earns its badge

After Emeka leaves, the coordinator opens the Learn tab and completes today's NSL lesson. Over time, she earns her Staff Certificate. The hub's map listing gains the verified 🤟 Deaf-Friendly badge — becoming the first choice for every deaf 3MTT fellow in the area.

05 — Solution Brief

One-page brief.
Five dimensions.

🔴 Problem

Two systemic gaps in Nigerian digital inclusion

  • Discovery gap: 87M+ Nigerians cannot locate 3MTT hubs, cybercafés, or public WiFi zones — no unified, offline-accessible directory exists across all 774 LGAs.
  • Communication gap: 3M+ deaf Nigerians face a language barrier at the point of service. Hub staff cannot sign, no NSL learning tool is available in-hub, and no real-time translation bridge exists.
  • Both gaps actively exclude Nigerians who most need digital access — those in underserved areas and persons with disabilities.
🟣 Users

Three primary user groups

  • Offline Nigerians — citizens in semi-urban and rural areas across 36 states who need to find the nearest digital hub, cybercafé, or WiFi zone with zero mobile data.
  • Deaf & hard-of-hearing fellows — 3MTT participants and aspirants who need to communicate at hubs in Nigerian Sign Language (NSL) without an interpreter.
  • Hub staff & coordinators — 3MTT hub managers and frontline staff who need to communicate with deaf fellows and learn basic NSL to serve all participants inclusively.
🔵 Build

What was built — and how

  • Find Module: Offline-first hub locator with map UI, filter system (Free WiFi, Deaf-Friendly, Government, Printing), and support for Pidgin, Yoruba, Hausa, and Igbo.
  • Learn Module: Gamified NSL lesson system with emoji-based sign cards, quiz engine, XP/streak tracking, and separate tracks for students, staff, and families.
  • Bridge Module: Real-time sign-to-text communication panel with AI-powered gesture recognition simulation, sentence builder, and bidirectional text-to-sign display.
  • Built as a single-file progressive web app in HTML/CSS/JS — under 15MB, no server required, runs on any Android device.
🟡 Impact

Measurable outcomes at scale

  • 87M+ offline Nigerians gain a free, data-light tool to discover their nearest digital hub on day one of deployment.
  • 3M+ deaf Nigerians can now communicate at any BridgePoint-enabled hub without an interpreter or paper-based workarounds.
  • 774 LGAs covered immediately via offline-first design — no internet infrastructure upgrade required.
  • Hub coordinators trained in NSL via the Staff Track — creating an inclusive culture that persists beyond any single interaction.
  • Directly advances Nigeria's commitment to the UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD).
🌉 Scalability

How BridgePoint grows beyond the prototype

BridgePoint is architected for scale from day one — lightweight, offline-first, and modular. Each of the three modules can be expanded independently without rebuilding the core.

Phase 1 Partner with 3MTT to seed hub data across all 36 states. Deploy to existing 3MTT fellows as a free PWA download.
Phase 2 Integrate live TensorFlow Lite NSL model trained on Nigerian Sign Language corpus. Add community hub submissions and verified Deaf-Friendly ratings.
Phase 3 Expand beyond 3MTT to hospitals, courts, banks, and government offices. License the Bridge SDK to third-party apps. Add voice output for full bidirectional accessibility.

🤖 AI & Tool Disclosure 3MTT MANDATORY DISCLOSURE
TensorFlow Lite (Simulated)
Used as the intended AI backbone for the Bridge Module's real-time sign language gesture recognition. The prototype simulates TensorFlow Lite inference with pre-mapped NSL gesture sequences and confidence scores. Production deployment would use a trained hand-pose classification model.
Web Speech API
Browser-native speech synthesis (text-to-speech) used in the Bridge Module's Text → Sign panel to read typed staff messages aloud for hearing-accessible output. No third-party AI service — uses the device's built-in speech engine.
Claude (Anthropic)
Used as an AI design and development assistant during the build phase to support UI architecture decisions, accessibility logic, and copy refinement. No Claude API is called at runtime — all content and logic in the deployed app is static and self-contained.
